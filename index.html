Data Protection & Privacy Notice
Effective Date: 19 March 2026
◈ 1. Who We Are

Firefly Cult Studios ("we", "us", "our") is an independent game development studio based in Wales, United Kingdom. We are the data controller for the personal information we collect through our websites, services, and testing programmes.

This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, and protect your personal data when you interact with any of our services, including but not limited to:

  • Our websites hosted on GitHub Pages
  • Pre-alpha and beta testing sign-up forms
  • Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA) signing pages
  • Our games and game-related services
  • Communications via email, Discord, or other channels

We are committed to protecting your privacy and handling your data in compliance with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR), the Data Protection Act 2018, and the US Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA).

⚑ 2. Age Requirement

Our services are intended for individuals aged 18 and over only. We do not knowingly collect, process, or store personal data from anyone under the age of 18.

If we become aware that we have collected personal data from an individual under 18, we will take immediate steps to delete that data from our systems. If you are a parent or guardian and believe your child has provided us with personal information, please contact us immediately at the address provided in Section 12.

This age restriction applies to all Firefly Cult Studios services, including game testing sign-ups, NDA signing, and any other data collection activities.

✦ 4. Legal Basis for Processing

Under the UK GDPR, we must have a lawful basis for processing your personal data. We rely on the following:

Purpose Lawful Basis
Processing your NDA signature and managing testing agreements Contractual necessity — Article 6(1)(b) UK GDPR
Administering pre-alpha tester sign-ups Consent — Article 6(1)(a) UK GDPR
Sending you testing-related communications Legitimate interest — Article 6(1)(f) UK GDPR
Complying with legal obligations Legal obligation — Article 6(1)(c) UK GDPR

Where we rely on consent, you may withdraw it at any time by contacting us. Withdrawal of consent does not affect the lawfulness of processing carried out before the withdrawal.

◈ 5. How We Use Your Data

We use your personal data for the following purposes:

  • To process and record your NDA signature and maintain a legally binding agreement
  • To manage your application to our pre-alpha testing programme
  • To contact you regarding testing access, build distribution, and programme updates
  • To verify your identity and eligibility for the testing programme
  • To respond to your enquiries or feedback
  • To comply with legal and regulatory obligations
  • To protect our rights and enforce our agreements

We will never:

  • Sell your personal data to third parties
  • Use your data for automated decision-making or profiling
  • Send you unsolicited marketing communications
  • Share your data with advertisers

◈ 7. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected:

Data Type Retention Period
NDA signature records For the duration of the NDA obligations plus 6 years (in line with the Limitation Act 1980 for contract claims under English law)
Testing sign-up data Until the testing programme concludes, or until you request deletion
Email correspondence Up to 2 years from last contact, unless related to an active agreement
Technical logs Up to 90 days (managed by hosting providers)

When data is no longer required, it will be securely deleted or anonymised.

✦ 8. Your Rights Under UK GDPR

Under the UK GDPR, you have the following rights regarding your personal data:

  • Right of access — You can request a copy of all personal data we hold about you (a "Subject Access Request").
  • Right to rectification — You can request that we correct any inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure — You can request that we delete your personal data, subject to any legal obligations that require us to retain it (e.g. NDA records).
  • Right to restrict processing — You can request that we limit how we use your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to data portability — You can request your data in a structured, commonly used, machine-readable format.
  • Right to object — You can object to processing based on legitimate interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent — Where processing is based on consent, you may withdraw it at any time.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the details in Section 12. We will respond within one calendar month of receiving your request, as required by law.

Note: Certain data (such as NDA signature records) may be exempt from erasure requests where retention is necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims.

⚑ 9. Data Security

We take the security of your personal data seriously and implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect it, including:

  • All data is transmitted over encrypted connections (HTTPS/TLS)
  • Database access is restricted using API key authentication and role-based access controls
  • Third-party processors are selected for their security practices and compliance certifications
  • Access to personal data is limited to authorised personnel only

While we take all reasonable steps to protect your data, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure. In the event of a data breach that poses a risk to your rights and freedoms, we will notify the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) within 72 hours and inform affected individuals without undue delay, in accordance with Articles 33 and 34 of the UK GDPR.

◈ 10. Cookies & Tracking

Our websites hosted on GitHub Pages are static sites. We do not use:

  • First-party cookies
  • Analytics or tracking scripts (no Google Analytics, no Meta Pixel, etc.)
  • Advertising cookies or tracking pixels
  • Fingerprinting or any form of cross-site tracking

Third-party services (such as Supabase) may use essential cookies or tokens for authentication purposes. These are strictly necessary for the functioning of the service and do not track your browsing activity.

✦ 11. Children's Privacy (COPPA Compliance)

In compliance with the US Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (COPPA), we affirm that:

  • Our services are not directed at children under the age of 13, nor at anyone under the age of 18.
  • We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.
  • We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 18.
  • If we discover that we have inadvertently collected data from a child under 13 (or under 18), we will delete it immediately and terminate any associated account or agreement.
  • We do not condition a child's participation in any activity on the disclosure of more personal information than is reasonably necessary.

Parents or guardians who believe their child may have submitted personal information to us should contact us immediately. We will take prompt action to investigate and delete the data.
